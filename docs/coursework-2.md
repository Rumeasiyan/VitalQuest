# The Task

1. Implement and provide user documentation of a prototype website for your company (50 marks)

a) Implement a working prototype of your website

Implement a functional website prototype for your business. The prototype should have the final navigation structure with the most important pages present, and all the necessary functions have to be indicated. However, the services that require further programming do not have to be implemented in their final form. For example, you can create a page for the shopping cart (if it is relevant) and simulate how it is working by creating sample pages. However, you do not have to actually implement this functionality with working code. Your website should include at least 10 different pages, based on the requirements and structure of your business scenario. Please concentrate on the most important and interesting pages that demonstrate your business idea and its unique selling points the best. Avoid implementing generic and simple pages, such as “About us” or “Contact us”. The focus should be on advanced functionalities and features that demonstrate the uniqueness of your business and differentiate you from your competitors. Consider the strategic and systems level decisions and ideas that you presented in your group coursework and if necessary, extend them.

It is advised that you use Google Sites for the implementation of the site. However, if you prefer, you may use any other website design and implementation tool that you are more familiar with. Please note that even if you select a more advanced and sophisticated tool than Google Sites, there will be no marks allocated for any technical implementation or programming. The emphasis should be on the demonstration of your advanced e-commerce ideas and not on the technical implementation. The marking scheme (see below) focuses on the design and implementation of your business ideas, and not on your technical programming or coding skills.

You do not have to submit anything directly as the outcome of this task. However, the implementation is necessary to create the user manual in task 1b, and you may also need to show your implementation to your tutor in case you are invited for a viva.

b) Write a User Manual for your application – max. 1,000 words and additional screenshots of every implemented page

Write a detailed user manual, illustrated with screenshots, and explain how your website works (or rather, will work after full implementation). Describe all pages and all functionalities in sufficient detail. Explain the limitations of the prototype highlighting all functionalities that will require coding and implementation in the final version of the site. Describe and explain the desired functionality of these components. Please note that limitations of your website in this sense are rather positive and demonstrate your advanced ideas that can be implemented and realised later. These limitations are typically not forced on you because of the website implementation tool selected but rather by the time and technical constraints of the task.

Please note that your tutors will mark Task 1 based on your user manual only, without checking your website (unless it is deemed necessary). Therefore, it is crucial that your user manual is clear and legible, and that you describe and illustrate all functionalities that you planned and implemented.

2. Analyse and assess how the results of your strategic analysis and systems design have been reflected in
   your prototype (25 marks) – max 700 words

Describe clearly and analyse how the strategic and systems level decisions and plans made in your Group Coursework have been implemented in your website. Select five representative examples from your strategic and systems level analysis in the Group Coursework and show how these results and outcomes of the different analytical techniques (e.g., Porter’s 5 Forces, SWOT, CSF, Use-case model, and other techniques you used) are demonstrated in your implemented prototype. When appropriate, illustrate with screenshots the connection between the design specification and the actual implementation. If you made changes and additional decisions when compared to your Group Coursework, please also explain and analyse why these changes were necessary.

For higher marks, select and focus on design decisions that led to more advanced functionalities and that demonstrate the uniqueness of your business idea.

3. Analyse and critically evaluate how your on-line business could utilise cloud computing (25 marks) – max
   700 words

Describe a cloud computing solution that could enrich your technical implementation and/or support your business processes. Analyse and critically evaluate how the selected cloud solution will be integrated to your business, what cost and security implications it may raise and what advantages/disadvantages the solution has on your business when compared to traditional IT infrastructures and services. Be as concrete as possible. You need to select and analyse a concrete solution available on the market, by a concrete provider. Categorise and justify the cloud computing service that will be applied (e.g. SaaS, PaaS, IaaS, private, public, hybrid etc.). Base your analysis and evaluation on related literature and correctly reference all your sources. Higher marks will only be awarded to answers where you clearly justify the specific relevance of the selected cloud solution to your own business and provide sufficient details explaining its role and utilisation by the business.
